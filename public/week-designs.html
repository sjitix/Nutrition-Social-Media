Direction 01

Timetable

Meal types as rows, days as columns — the way a real weekly plan is read. Scan one row to see every lunch this week; scan one column to see a full day. Dense, aligned, calm.

Best for at-a-glance scanning & comparing. Nothing is stretched — the grid gives structure, so it fills the width without voids. My recommendation for the primary week view.

Direction 02

Refined columns

The column idea done right: cards sized to their content, a slim calorie bar toward your daily target, tidy meal rows. Familiar, warm, and it breathes without gaping holes.

Best for a friendly, planner-like feel. Each day reads as a little agenda. Content-sized cards fix the empty-space problem the current version has.

Direction 03

Today agenda

Puts the day you actually cook front and center — full detail plus a calorie ring against target — with the rest of the week as a quiet rail beside it.

Best for daily use & the "what do I make now" moment. Great as the Home screen; pair it with 01 or 02 for the full-week view.

Direction 04

Dashboard

Leads with the numbers that matter — average calories, protein, adherence to target — then a condensed week strip. For when hitting macros is the whole point.

Best for the macro-tracking phase from your roadmap. Surfaces targets-vs-actual up top — the data-forward option once macro enforcement lands.
